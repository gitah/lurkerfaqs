mallow | Posted 8/30/2012 6:57:33 AM | message detail | quote
The reason I "did it again" is because I know my explanation is going to be extremely long and possibly completely fruitless in case you disagree with me so I'd rather you think about it for yourself first, but here goes:

Roger gained his title for doing the unprecedented feat of sailing the Grand Line completely. The reason Luffy or whoever else can also gain that title for themselves is because that highly prestigious feat has yet to be replicated, and doing so is still amazing enough taht the world would call you Pirate King if you reached Raftel and found One Piece. The "strongest" thing, in this era, will just be a byproduct of having to beat the fierce competition.

Being the strongest isn't enough. Whitebeard was actually known as the strongest man alive for like two decades but nobody dubbed him Pirate King because he never reached One Piece.

Luffy will do more than just inherit the title, he'll go a step beyond Roger and actually fulfill whatever One Piece's purpose is.

If there was, in the past, something else that would qualify you as "Pirate King" it would completely diminish Roger's accomplishment of being the first to conquer the Grand Line, creating the whole concept of being Pirate King (which to Roger and Luffy means having the most freedom, btw).. and subsequently diminish Luffy's future accomplishment of being the second, the last, and the greatest Pirate King for not only following Roger but then taking it a huge step further.

If you want something more concrete than my interpretation of this story then how about this... Luffy and Roger are extremely alike, and Luffy has known what he's wanted since he was a kid. If the same goes for Roger, then he would've said to Rayleigh that he would be the man to become the (next) Pirate King when they first met.
